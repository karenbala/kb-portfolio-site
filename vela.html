Play in All Kinds of Weather

Vela is a weather app that lets you play in the water.

Project Goals

Many weather applications provide weather forecasts to their users but not specific to watersports enthusiasts. Watersport users have different weather conditions based on their sport and tolerance for risk.
Vela provides users with accurate information so they can go out in the water with confidence.

Vela high fidelity prototypes: welcome screen, homescreen and a key feature, watersport play alert.

Working Problem Statement

Watersport enthusiasts need an easy solution to understand local weather conditions that are typically shown in complex technical charts so they can plan a safe and enjoyable time in the water.


Competition

Weather forecasts are mainly about applying sunscreen or grabbing your umbrella.

The Competition Space
The primary weather forecasters, weather.com, noaa.gov, and weather.gov have maintained their expertise and authority by being ubiquitous. Digital and non-digital weather outlets use one or all three organization’s information and satellites. Their information to their users are not specific or shared in way that informs water play.
An analogous analysis of Glossier, glossier.com, imparts wisdom to the competition space. They are a thriving company in the health and beauty space. It is a market that large name brand companies lead, but Glossier has created a loyal and enthusiasitic community. They do this by accepting their customers will have many brands of make-up in their bags: not all complexions are the same, and their community will drive their products.
Vela can be an addition to the top 3.

Weather.com is ubiquitous in the weather sector. Glossier is a new member of the health and beauty market.

Research Goals

What weather forecasting apps do users use before heading out into the water?
How often do they check weather apps before a trip? During the trip?
Have they ever misread a weather forecast in the past? How often? And what could be made better?

User Journeys

Aileen wants to play in the water.

Emphathizing with busy users connects the importance of a quick glance on a customized homescreen. Constantly, users are making quick decisions. The two points of confirmation: customized homescreen and play alerts helps a user like Aileen make better decisions.

User Persona: Aileen Butler

Trip planning can be very complex especially if weather conditions are part of a user’s fun. Understanding Nori’s need for weekly or daily confirmation about his future trip, a weather conditions notification feature would help ease packing burdens and have a safer trip in the water.

Prototype

Create Weather Play Alert

This feature was shared by all the users during the interview phase. The ability to set weather condition preferences so a user will receive an alert when optimal weather conditions align so they can go play in the water.

Top and bottom navigation buttons do not clutter the map view.

Users can control their preferences in the modal.

The user can create a new name for their alert, watersport of choice and weather conditions.

Customize Homepage Weather Views

This feature allows a user to customize their homepage screen to show only the weather conditions they care about. During user interviews this was an important feature that is not available with popular applications.

Users can scroll and toggle their preferences in this modal.

The homepage with views of selected conditions to display.
